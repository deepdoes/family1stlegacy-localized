Common Questions

Frequently Asked
Questions.

Family First Legacy helps individuals, families, and business owners understand their options for life insurance, retirement planning, education planning, and legacy planning. We explain things clearly so you can make informed decisions at your own pace.

Life insurance may be more affordable than many people expect. The cost depends on age, health, coverage amount, product type, and eligibility.

Life insurance is not just for parents. It may help cover final expenses, debts like a mortgage, or support people who depend on you. Some policies may also include living benefits for certain covered illnesses or conditions.

Living Benefits may allow qualified policyholders to access part of their policy benefits while still living if they experience certain covered illnesses or conditions. These funds may help with needs such as bills, medical costs, or lost income. Availability depends on the policy and eligibility.

IUL is life insurance first, not a direct stock market investment. It may help protect your family while building cash value linked to a market index, depending on the policy design. Many IUL policies include a 0% floor on index interest credits, which can help reduce the impact of negative index performance. Costs, caps, limits, and policy rules apply.

A 401(k) can be a valuable retirement tool, but it may not cover every retirement need by itself. Traditional 401(k) withdrawals are generally taxable, and your account value can go up or down with the market. Some families also explore annuities or life insurance strategies to help add principal protection, income options, and balance to their retirement plan.

Some annuities are designed to help protect your principal from market-index losses while still giving you an opportunity to earn interest when the index performs well. Because each product works differently, we explain the details clearly so you understand the protection, growth potential, and rules before making a decision.

Probate can delay access to certain assets when a family may need money most. Life insurance with the right named beneficiary may help provide funds directly to loved ones for bills, funeral costs, or daily needs, while other assets are still being handled. For wills, trusts, and legal estate planning, we encourage families to work with a qualified legal professional.

Permanent life insurance that builds cash value can offer flexible access through policy loans or withdrawals. Unlike a 529 plan, which is designed mainly for qualified education expenses, cash value may be used for college or other needs if your child’s path changes. We help you understand how to use this strategy carefully before deciding if it fits your family.
